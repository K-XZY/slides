Self-supervised RL

"Can we reformulate RL as Generative AI? That is, from control to probability?"
— Eysenbach, Amy Zhang et al.
VLA ⊂ GCRL ⊂ RL ⊂ GenAI
FUNDAMENTAL THEOREM
\(\displaystyle f^*(s_t, a_t, s_g) = \log \frac{p^\pi(s_{\delta t+} = s_g \mid s_t, a_t)}{p(s_g)} + c\)
No reward function · No Bellman backup · No target network
Basic Adaptation
Substitute TD3 / DDPG's Q-function with the joint embedding model.
Train via contrastive loss on trajectory data — no reward engineering needed.
Step 1 — GCRL density formulation
Reward as probability. Objective = maximizing the discounted state occupancy measure (DSOM):
\(r_g(s,a) = (1\!-\!\gamma)\, p(s_{t+1}\!=\!g \mid s,a)\)
\(\displaystyle \max_{\pi}\; p^{\pi}(s_{t+}\!=\!g \mid s_0, a_0) \;=\; \max_{\pi}\; (1\!-\!\gamma)\, \mathbb{E}_{\pi}\!\Big[\sum_{t=0}^{\infty} \gamma^t\, p(s_{t+1}\!=\!g \mid s_t, a_t)\Big]\)
Step 2 — Relative DSOM
Direct estimation is hard. Use density ratio instead — the optimal classifier \(f_\theta\):
\(\displaystyle \arg\max_a \frac{p^{\pi}(s_{t+}\!=\!g \mid s,a)}{p(g)} \;=\; f_\theta(s, a, g)\)
Step 3 — Q = Contrastive Learning
GCRL's Q-function \(\equiv f_\theta\), parametrized in log-linear form (joint embedding):
\(f_\theta(s, a, g) = \exp\!\big(\theta(s,a)^T \!\cdot\! \psi(g)\big)\)
Eysenbach et al. 2022 (CRL) · ICML 2025 Tutorial (Eysenbach & Amy Zhang)

↓ state of the art

Core CRL insight: Q-function = density estimation = contrastive learning. Three-step derivation from GCRL density formulation to the fundamental theorem. Practical: substitute Q in TD3/DDPG with joint embedding model.

Part 3

Leveraging the New Math

Concrete research questions from CRL → VLA

Q1
Can we show VLAC and ViCLIP follow a variation of absolute-DSOM?
Prove or disprove that VLAC is inherently performing contrastive learning in the CRL sense. If yes, VLAC's reward = CRL's Q-function up to a constant.
Q2
Do temporal contrastive learning assumptions hold when language is the goal?
Given a dataset of VLA triplets \((s_t, a_t, l_{\text{task}})\), does the CRL framework still apply with \(g = l\)?
Q3
Do the three conditions in "Law of the Unconscious Contrastive Learner" hold in VLA?
What can be done to make them hold? This determines whether CRL's guarantees transfer to VLA settings.

↓ vague questions & next steps

Three concrete questions: (1) Is VLAC = CRL's absolute-DSOM? (2) Does CRL work with language goals? (3) Do the Law's three conditions hold for VLA? The framework: math intuition ↔ semantic intuition ↔ empirical observation.
